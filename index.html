The Mansaka

Preserving the Heritage of the Uplands

Ethnolinguistic Background

Etymologically derived from man (person) and saka (to ascend), the Mansaka are recognized as the "people of the uplands." They constitute one of the eighteen distinct Lumad ethnolinguistic groups in Mindanao.

Geographically, their population is concentrated in the provinces of Davao de Oro and Davao del Norte, specifically inhabiting the Maragusan Valley. The Mansaka are historically noted for their distinct physiological features and an egalitarian social structure governed by elders known as Matikadong.

Cultural Identity & Arts

The cultural heritage of the Mansaka is defined by their agrarian lifestyle, intricate beadwork, and distinctive textile production.

The Dagmay Cloth

A culturally significant abaca textile featuring intricate geometric patterns. Traditionally, these designs are not drafted but are revealed to the weaver through dreams.

Musical Tradition

Their auditory culture utilizes instruments such as the Kudlong (two-stringed lute) and the Gimbal (drum) to communicate narratives and folklore.

Strategic Advocacy & Support

To ensure the preservation of Mansaka identity and rights, our group proposes the following support mechanisms:

1. Ancestral Domain

Promoting literacy regarding the Indigenous Peoples' Rights Act (IPRA) to protect ancestral lands (CADT) from unauthorized encroachment and industrial exploitation.

2. Economic Empowerment

Facilitating ethical trade by connecting Dagmay weavers directly to markets, eliminating intermediaries to ensure fair economic compensation.

3. Cultural Integrity

Digitizing oral traditions and establishing guidelines for responsible eco-tourism to prevent the commodification of their sacred rituals.
